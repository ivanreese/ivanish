Creative work of Ivan Reese, by type or by time.
Organs

March 16th, 2013

I wrote 9 songs in an evening, and boasted about it on the internet. It felt good.

At the time I was pobsessed with the artwork of Jessica YH, and in the final throws of my friendship with Kiri. She'd fled our house, Supermoon Mansion, to go live with her parents for the umpteenth. I made these songs in her half-emptied bedroom as a quiet act of revolt.

But I should have stayed out of her room. The acoustics were fucking bleak.

The lyrics for these songs came from a succession of dreams — which is always a cop-out, if you'll pardon the pun — about Kiri's ex, Jessica's characters, the Calgary Police, and the usual flying and unusual imagery of typical dreamstuff. I was well in the habit of writing it all down, a habit I've since lost. No wonder I haven't made any albums since.

March 17th, 2013

Making my way down the staircase in the morning, I was punched in the stomach by a ghost, and had to catch myself on the banister. I felt horrible. It was my first full day of vertigo, one of many over the following few months. Instead of supper that evening, I dragged myself down the hill to the Roasterie for tea and fresh air. Freyja was there, my still-secret crush, and I tried to describe it to her by quoting my favourite TV show. I told her I feel like I'm being beaten up underwater.

These recurring episodes of dizziness culminated in a Psychic Alliance show, where afterward, I sat in my car throbbing for an hour and a half, unable to see and constantly about to vomit, scared that if I tried to drive home I'd kill myself. It left me deaf in the left ear to all sounds above the middle register. The "ear, nose, and throat" specialist who spent all of 5 minutes with me probably used the money to turn himself orange, that vacationing asshole, and told me nothing could help restore my natural hearing.

October 10th, 2015

Two and a half years later, I'm releasing a handful of those 9 songs. I haven't managed any music since that Spring, so I feel like I might as well accept that this album is never going to be finished.
